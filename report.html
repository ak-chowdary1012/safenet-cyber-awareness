If it happens, act fast.

The first hour after a cyber fraud matters most because banks can often freeze a transaction before it's withdrawn. Here's exactly what to do and who to contact.

National Cyber Crime Helpline
1930

Call this number immediately for financial frauds involving UPI, banking, cards, or wallets. Available 24/7 across India.

Step by step

What to do after a cyber fraud

Follow these steps in order. Speed matters most for financial frauds.

1

Call 1930 immediately

This is India's dedicated helpline for reporting financial cyber fraud. Acting within the "golden hour" significantly increases the chance of recovering funds before they're withdrawn by the scammer.

2

File a complaint on cybercrime.gov.in

Visit the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal and file a detailed complaint. For financial fraud, choose "Report Financial Fraud." For other crimes (harassment, hacking, etc.), use "Report Other Cyber Crimes."

3

Inform your bank

Call your bank's official helpline (printed on your card/passbook) to report the fraudulent transaction and request it be blocked or reversed if still pending. Ask for a written acknowledgement.

4

Preserve all evidence

Take screenshots of messages, transaction IDs, call logs, and any links involved before they disappear. Note down the date, time, and amount because this is critical for the investigation.

5

Change passwords & secure accounts

If credentials were compromised, immediately change passwords for affected accounts and enable two-factor authentication. Check for any unauthorised logins or linked devices.

6

Visit your local cyber cell (if needed)

For larger frauds or identity theft cases, visit your nearest cyber crime police station with printed copies of your complaint, ID proof, and evidence to file a formal FIR.

Before you call

Information to have ready

  • Transaction ID / UTR number (for financial fraud)
  • Date, time, and amount of the transaction
  • Screenshots of messages, calls, or emails
  • Phone number or account used by the scammer
  • Your bank account number and registered mobile
  • A brief written timeline of what happened
