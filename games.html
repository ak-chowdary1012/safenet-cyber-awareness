Practice makes paranoid (in a good way)

Three short games that train the same instincts real scams try to bypass — spotting red flags, building strong passwords, and checking links before you click.

Click every suspicious word or phrase

Found: 0 / 7

This message claims to be from a bank. Click on every word, phrase, or link that you think is a red flag. Some highlighted text is just normal — only click what feels suspicious.

URGENT: Dear valued customer, this is to inform you that your account has been flagged for review and will be BLOCKED within 24 hours unless you verify-now by visiting http://secure-update-bank.in. You will be asked to confirm your OTP and registered mobile number. Please act immediately to avoid suspension of all banking services. Thank you for banking with us.
✓"URGENT" in caps — designed to trigger panic and rushed decisions.
✓"BLOCKED" — fear-based language pressuring immediate action.
✓"within 24 hours" — artificial deadline to prevent careful thinking.
✓"verify-now" — vague call to action pushing you toward a link.
✓The link uses HTTP and a generic domain — not your real bank's official site.
✓Asking to "confirm OTP" — no bank ever needs your OTP to "verify" anything.
✓"act immediately" — another urgency trigger to bypass careful thinking.
